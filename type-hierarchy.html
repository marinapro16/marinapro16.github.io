TYPE HIERARCHY

The Black Experience In Design

For this project in my Typography I class, I practiced typography using the book The Black Experience in Design. I began by collecting essential text elements from the book, such as the title, editors, publishers, forewords, and featured content.

Initially, I arranged the text on a square page with columns and a grid, experimenting with placement, line spacing, indents, and alignment. As I progressed, I added font weight and italics to enhance the text hierarchy.

With a solid grasp of typography principles, I incorporated design elements like shapes, scale, and rotation. Towards the end, I added imagery and color, thinking deeply to ensure they represented the project well and had meaningful reasons behind their inclusion. This refinement achieved a balanced look between text and visuals.
